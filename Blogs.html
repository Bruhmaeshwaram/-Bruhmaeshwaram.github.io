Blog#2:The Eudaimonia Machine

Before I start off, I would like to introduce two concepts because well If I do not, then you might not understand anything about today‚Äôs topic.
**Deep work**: *Professional activities performed in a state of distraction free concentration that push your cognitive capabilities to their limit. These efforts create new value, improve your skill, and are hard to replace.* For Example- Something that you could relate to is doing hard math problems, or writing research papers, these tasks require to intensely concentrate and make you think to your maximum ability thereby producing work that is hard to replace and of value.
**Shallow work**: *Non-cognitively demanding, logistical-style tasks, often performed while distracted. These efforts tend to not create much new value in the world and are easy to replicate.* For example- Replying to emails, Data entry, work that doesn‚Äôt produce any value and can be easily replicated by anyone.
So now, for today‚Äôs topic, the eudaimonia machine
The Greek Concept of ‚Äòeudaimonia‚Äô(a state where you are achieving your full human potential) inspires the Eudaimonia Machine. A space designed for the sole purpose of enabling the deepest possible deep work.
Since deep work requires intense concentration and a distraction free environment, this concept of a place where you can achieve the deepest possible deep work comes from a Chicago based architect, David Dewane.
It is a linear series of room or stages where one cannot reach the next level/room without going through the first.
First, **The gallery**
: This room would contain examples of deep work produced by the organization. It is meant to inspire employees and to create a culture of healthy stress and peer pressure.
Second, **The salon**: This room would contain access to high-quality coffee and perhaps a cafe. It would be furnished with couches and Wi-Fi. The salon is intended to create a mood somewhere between intense curiosity and argumentation. This is a place to debate, brood, and generally work through the ideas that will be developed deeper in the machine.
Third, **The library**: This room stores a permanent record of all work, as well as previous books and resources used by the organization. The room would contain copiers and scanners for gathering and collecting information needed for a project. The library should be considered the hard drive of the machine.
Fourth, **The office space**: The office space would contain a standard conference room and cubicles with desks. The office space is intended for low-intensity activity. This is the space designated to complete the less demanding efforts required for a project. An administrator with a desk in the office space could help occupants improve their work habits to optimize their efficiency.
Fifth, **The chamber**: The final room of the machine is a collection of deep work chambers. Each chamber is intended to be six by ten feet and protected by soundproof walls. The purpose of the deep work chamber is to allow for total focus and uninterrupted workflow.
(Source- Deep work by Cal Newport)
So, how it goes is that once you go through the first 4 stages which compromise mostly of shallow work efforts for your project, you go into the chambers for about 90 minutes and then a 90-minute break and repeat the process 2-3 times a day since performing deep work is mentally straining and challenging.
Now, why would we need something like this at all? Our offices have private cabins and privacy is always available. The truth is, that we as an economy are moving towards and starting a ubiquitous culture of connectivity. A place where your ‚Äòbusyness‚Äô online is seen as your contribution and that always being online during work is necessary for you to reach your potential. That, these tools such as slack and teams will help you work better and contribute more. Mindlessly surrounding yourself with distractions while trying to achieve your best potential. üòÇ
To escape this rather hostile culture that is being popularized in the workplace, this idea of a Eudaimonia machine seems like a ray of hope for when companies may realize that deep work would profit them! The segments clearly replicate the stages you need to go through to get into a state of deep work one by one to achieve the final state i.e., when you are distraction-free, concentrating and pushing yourself to your limit to produce work which is extremely valuable.
Even though it does not seem like we would get something like this in our workplace anytime in the future, the idea of a place where one can achieve their potential in a distraction-free environment and keep working to produce at their very best level is insane!

Blog#3:Exams

As Exam season approaches for some of us, tensions and anxiety are on an ever high. It‚Äôs kind of important to understand that Exams are just a tool to help us gauge our understanding of a particular topic/subject. As Shakespeare wrote in Hamlet
‚ÄòThere is nothing good or bad, but thinking makes it so.‚Äô
Exams are just a piece of paper we give meaning to. While a bad exam may not be the end of the road for you, You just have to work hard and we‚Äôll all have a good future and succeed.
So, here are some tips I think can be useful while preparing for an Exam:
We have to understand that the night before an exam is not really about revision but about setting yourself up for the big day. Some things to keep in mind are:-
- Sleep- Sleep is the time where memory is consolidated and new connections are made. Therefore, it‚Äôs kind of important that you get the optimal time of sleep not only to be refreshed but also for the memory to be consolidated. So sleep early and try not to cram up at nightüòÑ
- The night before an exam is no longer about understanding. You want to optimize time by using Active recall techniques like blurting( literally means to blurt out whatever you know and fixing the gaps in your knowledge) and flashcards. The best thing to do would be start applying the information you would have learnt, Start completing past paper questions or practice worksheets and basically tons of questions. Trying to create an exam like scenario beforehand makes exams feel less scary.
- For peace of mind, consider making yourself a last-minute cheat sheet with all the niggling point you want to go over in the final moments. The process of making it useful to pick out what you really don‚Äôt know and rereading it on the day gives you peace of mind.
- If you‚Äôre usually unprepared for an exam due to the stress and anxiety the night before, First, plan your wake-up alarm accordingly, and make a checklist for all the resources you might need for the exam. Make sure to pack your bag the night before and cross check a couple of times if you have everything you need.
- If you‚Äôre struggling to sleep, First, get off your phone or laptop and stop panicking in the group chat. Secondly, have a proper bedtime wind-down, take your mind off revision once you‚Äôre done and just do an exercise that would clear your head. Third, find a way to express how you‚Äôre feeling before since exam season is not a sprint but a(long and evil) marathon.
- An underused mindfulness technique is positive visualization. Based on a branch of human memory called Hebbian learning. The basic idea is that the more you think of something, the easier it is to think the same thing again. The reason this is so powerful is because it lets you break old ways of thinking about exams and take conscious control over your approach. It allows you to make your brain a friend, a trusty accomplice, rather than an enemy.
An example would be to Imagine yourself in the exam hall slowly going through the paper and looking and reading at each question with a deep feeling of: *I‚Äôve got this*
Imagine yourself finding a question that you don‚Äôt understand and instead of panicking calmly working through it using whatever knowledge you do have. Imagine yourself being logical, trusting that somewhere in your brain you have the jigsaw pieces to get somewhere on this question. It‚Äôs something new for me too so we might as well try itüòÄ.
All these tips and tricks are from a book I absolutely loved and would recommend:

We love to be challenged and enjoy a certain activity when it pushes us beyond the level of our current ability. Applying this concept to our lives, we must always try to go a step further, try to challenge ourselves in our areas of interest or for pushing yourself out of your comfort zone. Only when our brain is pushed to its limits, we feel satisfaction of completion and accomplishment.

Blog#5:New Year New me?


As 2022 comes to an end, It‚Äôs the time of the year to get make some resolutions for the coming year, right? I mean everyone does make a resolution but how many of us complete it? Studies suggest that only about 15% of the people stick to their goals and see some considerable changes in their lifestyle. But why is this? Why are we unable to go through and complete the very resolutions we make, the changes we want to see in our lives? There could be multiple reasons like your goal could be unrealistic or you do not know why you want to achieve your goal or even that you‚Äôre bored of the process. The problem here is that setting a goal is easy, but accomplishing it requires self-discipline. Here‚Äôs a famous quote from James Clear In Atomic Habits about this very problem.
‚ÄúYou do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems.

Rather than setting a goal, and just winging it, Create a system. Thus, this time if you want to set a resolution, try, and create a system to help yourself achieve it! All the best and See you next yearüòÅ.
